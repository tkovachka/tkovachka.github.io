I'm currently in the highs of experimenting.

I pick up projects in technologies I've barely touched before - this website is a good example, built in Astro with no prior
Astro experience - because I've learned to **rely on AI agents** and structured workflows to fill the gaps. It works out. 
What I care about is the **exploration** itself: the research phase, the architectural decisions, the moment a new technology **clicks**.

I constantly find myself overflowing with **ideas**. In the past I thought that I would have to choose which ideas to pursue, which to let go.
Now I don't have those limitations. Because I know that all I have to do is make a good plan and let AI do the work for me.
Now I can **take a chance** on each idea I get. And that is a **freedom** I am enjoying thoroughly. 

The bottom-line in everything I build is **automation**. I'm genuinely passionate about
designing systems that remove friction - not just for users, but for myself. If a task
is repetitive, I want a process for it. If a decision is routine, I want a model
handling it. That philosophy shapes how I write code, how I run my projects, and how I
think about what software is for.

At the end of the day - I'm still figuring out where all of this leads - and I think
that's exactly the right place to be right now.
